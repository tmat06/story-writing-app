## Differentiation thesis
This stands out when we make feedback-to-revision a first-class workflow inside drafting, not just a comment inbox. Inkitt-style feedback is market-familiar, but converting clustered feedback directly into revision tasks and weighted chapter-level decisions creates a writer-first execution loop competitors don’t emphasize.

## Competitor evidence
- Inkitt positions itself around reader engagement and chapter-level feedback loops, validating demand for collecting reader signals: https://www.inkitt.com/writer-s-blog/how-does-inkitt-work-for-authors/
- LivingWriter emphasizes structured drafting (chapters/scenes, organization) and AI-assisted writing workflows, but does not foreground a beta-reader digest-to-task pipeline in core positioning: https://livingwriter.com/ and https://guides.livingwriter.com/product-documentation/ai-features
- Campfire’s differentiation is deep worldbuilding modules and lore management rather than operationalizing external reader feedback into revision execution: https://www.campfirewriting.com/features/worldbuilding

## Risks / trade-offs
- Theme bucketing and confidence scoring can over-normalize nuanced feedback and hide minority-but-important signals.
- Trust controls (excluding outliers/anonymous input) can introduce bias if defaults are too aggressive.
- Scope can expand quickly if clustering quality and export formats are treated as v1 perfection targets.

## Recommendation
Approve with a design brief that prioritizes: transparency of clustering logic, fast drill-down to source comments, and a low-friction "convert to revision task" interaction that works in under 2 clicks from a digest cluster.
