## Decision
approve

## Differentiation thesis
This feature stands out when positioned as **continuous narrative alignment inside drafting**, not just planning metadata. Competitors offer planning boards/tags, but keeping premise, stakes, and setup/payoff promises visible and actionable at scene time reduces drift in the writing moment.

## Competitor evidence
- LivingWriter emphasizes planning structures and element management (planning grid for plot points/scenes and element tags/relationships), but this is primarily organization-first rather than a dedicated in-draft promise tracker: https://guides.livingwriter.com/product-documentation/boards/planning-board/standard-grid and https://guides.livingwriter.com/product-documentation/chapters-and-elements/elements-section
- Novelcrafter supports labels and matrix/codex tracking across scenes, and explicitly notes there is no dedicated place for a book summary (as of Nov 2024), indicating a gap in always-on story-level guidance while drafting: https://docs.novelcrafter.com/en/articles/8677718-labeling-your-scenes , https://docs.novelcrafter.com/en/articles/8675733-the-plan-interface , https://docs.novelcrafter.com/en/articles/9419387-where-do-i-put-my-scene-chapter-act-book-summary-what-about-my-beats
- Campfire highlights modular tools and note sidebars in manuscript workflows, but messaging is broad module flexibility rather than explicit promise-to-scene accountability: https://www.campfirewriting.com/ and https://www.campfirewriting.com/pricing

## Risks / trade-offs
- Extra scene-tagging friction can hurt drafting flow if interaction cost is too high.
- Promise status semantics (`open/in progress/resolved`) can become inconsistent without clear UX constraints.
- If over-specified, this can feel like prescriptive plotting instead of writer support.

## Recommendation
Proceed, but anchor MVP on fast in-editor interactions: one-click scene alignment tags, frictionless collapse/expand, and low-cognitive-load promise tracking. Success should be measured by reduced unaligned scenes and fewer late revision loops, not just panel usage.
