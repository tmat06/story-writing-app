# Market Research: Summary and Improvement Plan

## What Market Research Does Today

- **Reviews** feature tickets from Marketing Product and approves or filters them before they go to engineering.
- **Assigns** approved tickets to the Founding Engineer so they appear in the Engineer’s inbox.
- **Shares** competitor and market insights with Marketing Product so they can write better tickets.
- **Uses** docs/INSPIRATION.md and the company goal as reference; outputs feedback, approvals, and research-style tickets (no code).

In practice: Market Research is the **gate and strategy layer** between “ideas” (Marketing) and “build” (Engineer). They decide what gets into the engineering backlog and add context from the market.

---

## Where It Falls Short (and How to Improve)

Today the role is mostly **reactive**: approve/filter and share insights. To make this step the one that **makes the app stand out from competition**, Market Research should be more **proactive and differentiation-focused**.

| Gap | Improvement |
|-----|-------------|
| **No explicit “stand out” mandate** | Make differentiation the primary lens: “Why would a writer choose us over Living Writer, Campfire, or Inkitt?” Every approval and recommendation should tie to that. |
| **Approve/filter only** | Add **shaping**: when reviewing tickets, add or suggest concrete “differentiation angles” (e.g. “Do X like Living Writer but simpler” or “Avoid Y because Campfire does it and users find it noisy”). Feed these to Marketing and, when approving, to the ticket description so the Engineer knows *why* it matters. |
| **Insights are ad hoc** | Produce **short, recurring outputs**: e.g. “Competitor pulse” (what competitors do well / where they fall short) and “Differentiation opportunities” (2–3 concrete areas where we can do better). Store or summarize in a doc or ticket so Marketing and the CEO can use it. |
| **No clear bar for “good” tickets** | Define a simple bar: a ticket is worth approving if it (a) advances the product toward the goal and (b) either matches or improves on competition (no blind copy; prefer “better than X” or “we do Y because they don’t”). Reject or send back tickets that are generic or don’t support differentiation. |
| **Weak link to INSPIRATION.md** | Use docs/INSPIRATION.md as the **baseline** (what we’re inspired by). Market Research should go one step further: identify **gaps and differentiators** (e.g. “Living Writer is strong on structure but weak on Z; we should own Z”). |

---

## Target State: Market Research as the Differentiation Engine

- **Inputs:** Feature tickets from Marketing Product, company goal, docs/INSPIRATION.md, and (over time) competitor/market notes.
- **Core question:** “Does this help us stand out in a positive way, or are we just matching the market?”
- **Outputs:**  
  - Approved tickets (assigned to Engineer) with brief “differentiation note” in the ticket or comment (why this matters vs. competition).  
  - Rejected or sent-back tickets with clear reason and, when useful, a suggestion for how to reframe (e.g. “Turn this into a differentiation opportunity by…”).  
  - Occasional research tickets or summaries: “Competitor pulse” and “Differentiation opportunities” so the rest of the org stays aligned.
- **No code:** Market Research only recommends and approves; the Engineer implements.

This keeps the same flow (Marketing → Market Research → Engineer) but makes Market Research the step that **forces positive, differentiation-driven decisions** so the app stands out instead of blending in.
