# Reference products

Use these apps as inspiration for the story-writing app. **Living Writer** is the primary reference.
The quality bar is: *would a writer who uses iA Writer or Bear feel at home here?*

---

## Living Writer (primary)

- **Focus:** Novel writing with structure and a clean drafting experience.
- **Take inspiration from:** Doc-style editor + strong organization (chapters, scenes, drag-and-drop); sidebar/corkboard for structure; timeline and outline support; optional AI for outlines, analysis, and element generation; privacy-first (opt-in AI, no training on user content). Prioritize a smooth writing flow and clear story structure over extra complexity.

---

## iA Writer

- **Focus:** Distraction-free writing. The gold standard for focused prose.
- **Take inspiration from:** Pure focus mode, typography-first design, minimal chrome, content-width constraints for readable line lengths, subtle but satisfying micro-interactions, keyboard-first UX. Nothing competes with the writing itself.

---

## Bear

- **Focus:** Notes and writing with excellent typography and organization.
- **Take inspiration from:** Clean sidebar navigation, hashtag-based organization, smooth scrolling and transitions, warm grayscale aesthetic, inline preview, compact and readable UI density.

---

## Campfire

- **Focus:** Worldbuilding and fiction with linked story elements.
- **Take inspiration from:** Dedicated modules for worldbuilding (locations, cultures, languages, magic, species, maps); notes sidebar with tagged story elements; panels and organization that keep world context visible while writing. Consider how to surface characters, places, and rules without cluttering the main editor.

---

## Craft

- **Focus:** Document creation with block-based editing and beautiful defaults.
- **Take inspiration from:** Block structure that feels fluid not clunky; cards and nested pages; excellent empty-state design; polish in every interaction. Study how it handles hierarchy.

---

## Notion

- **Focus:** Flexible workspace with blocks, databases, and views.
- **Take inspiration from:** Multiple views of the same content (table, board, gallery, calendar); inline databases for tracking characters/locations/timelines; breadcrumb navigation in nested structures. Avoid Notion's complexity — take the structural ideas, not the kitchen-sink approach.

---

## Inkitt

- **Focus:** Writing community and reader feedback.
- **Take inspiration from:** Reader engagement, feedback on chapters, and community around stories. Later phases could consider sharing, feedback loops, or discovery — not required for the core writing experience.

---

## What to search for when researching

When doing web research before writing a ticket or design brief, use searches like:

- `"story writing app" UX 2024 2025`
- `novel writing software comparison`
- `"writing app" distraction free focus mode`
- `scrivener alternatives writers`
- `writing app typography readability`
- `[specific feature] writing app example`
- `iA Writer Bear Craft design patterns`
- `fiction writing app corkboard scene cards`

Cite at least 2–3 specific findings (with source) in every ticket's `## Research` section and every design brief's `## Design research` section.

---

When in doubt, bias toward Living Writer's clarity and writer-first flow. Never ship something that looks like a generic web app.
